49. Spatial and genetic patterns of crane flies (Diptera: Tipuloidea) from Lake Hovsgol region, Mongolia
Citations: 3
Authors:Bazartseren Boldgiv
Publication_date: 2006
Publication: Many studies have focused on spatial isolation and its consequence in geographical structure of populations. Studies of this kind usually find a significant effect of isolation by distance by mostly examining a single species and the observed genetic pattern is usually attributed to limited dispersal. However, observed spatial genetic patterns provide information on cumulative effects of forces acting over many generations. The first part of this thesis examined the spatial genetic patterns in four crane fly species (Diptera: Tipuloidea) co-occurring in six Lake Hövsgöl tributary valleys, Mongolia: Arctotipula hovsgolensis, Beringotipula unca, Metalimnobia quadrinotata and Nephrotoma erebus. Observed differences in spatial patterns of these related species then can be explained by differences in the biology of the species.
