24. Feature Recognition through Artificial Intelligence Planning
Citations: 
Authors:Martin G Marchetta, Raymundo Q Forradellas
Publication_date: Machining feature recognition has been identified as a key activity for integrating the output of CAD systems with the so-called downstream applications, specially with Computer Aided Process Planning (CAPP). CAPP is considered the bridge between design and manufacturing, so the integration between CAD and CAPP is very important in the context of CIM systems. CAD and CAPP systems are concerned about different issues (namely design and manufacturing), so feature recognition is useful as an interface between them. Most feature recognition systems either are difficult to adapt and extend to specific industries and cases, or they have limitations with respect to the features they can recognize. In this paper, a declarative model based on artificial intelligence planning and the STEP standard is presented, that overcomes these limitations and is easily extensible and adaptable at the same time.

26. A Descriptive Model of the Wine Supply Chain
Citations: 
Authors:Fernanda A Garcia, Martín G Marchetta, Raymundo Q Forradellas
Publication_date: In a competitive and turbulent environment, companies of all industries are realizing the importance of counting with models that represent supply chains’ dynamics, in order to have a better understanding of the performance of key processes all along the supply network. Knowing the performance it is easier to find opportunities to improve it. So far, little research in holistic wine supply chain models has been developed. In this paper a representative model for the wine supply chain is proposed, which includes actors, integration of roles in the chain, information and material flows, potential issues that could arise, and some indicators used to measure the performance in logistic processes. An instance of the proposed model for a specific winery from Argentina is presented as a case study, in order to illustrate how to apply the generic supply chain model to specific cases.

27. Un modelo basado en el conocimiento para la planificación de procesos asistida por computadora
Citations: 
Authors:MG Marchetta, RQ Forradellas
Publication_date: La planificación de procesos asistida por computadora (CAPP, Computer Aided Process Planning) ha sido reconocida como el vínculo que permite reducir la brecha entre el diseño y la manufactura asistidos por computadora (CAD y CAM respectivamente). Diversos enfoques y técnicas para obtener sistemas de CAPP han sido propuestos e implementados. La mayoría de ellos, sin embargo, tienen la desventaja de ser difíciles de extender para soportar un espectro mayor de productos, y de adaptar a diversas industrias. En este trabajo se presenta un modelo basado en el conocimiento que atenúa estas desventajas, brindando al mismo tiempo posibilidades similares en cuanto a la aplicabilidad.
