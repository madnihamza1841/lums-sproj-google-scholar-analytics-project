282. EVALUACIÓN DE LA CALIDAD DE CONSENTIMIENTOS INFORMADOS DE LOS SERVICIOS Y/O HOSPITALES DE III-IV NIVEL DE LIMA Y CALLLAO
Citations: 
Authors:Germán Málaga Rodriguez, Gabriela Tupayachi Ortiz, Tania Guevara Guevara, Fernando Hidalgo Salinas
Publication_date: Objective:
To evaluate the quality of the Consent Forms for Major Surgery used in the different services of hospitals of III–IV level of Lima and Callao.
Material and methods:
Descriptive, qualitative and transversal study. Once obtained the Consent Forms used for Major Surgery in III and IV level Hospitals of Lima and Callao, the quality of the different Consent Forms was evaluated according to the requirements established from a research in medical literature.
Results:
From 28 Consent Forms of 20 hospitals of the III-IV level of Lima and Callao analyzed, 14.2% fulfilled more than 75% of the requirements; 57.2%, between 75-50%; and 28.6%, less than 50%. The requirements about the procedure and its characteristics were absent in more than 75% of the forms. The Consent Forms from the hospitals of the Peruvian Health Department fulfilled an average of 12 requirements (in a range of 4-21); the ones from the Military Forces, 17 (range 14-18); and the ones from the Social Security Services, 18 (range 16-22).
Conclusions:
The Consent Forms for Major Surgery used at the different services of hospitals of level III-IV of Lima and Callao are not harmonized and do not guarantee that the patient has been informed completely, nor respected his autonomy at adopting his decision. The Consent Forms from the hospitals of the Peruvian Health Department were the most deficient and the ones with more quality variability. We propose a Consent Form Model for future Consent Forms.

Citations: 
Authors:Sergio Vasquez-Kunze, Germán Málaga
Publication_date: Las Guías de Practica Clínica (GPC) constituyen piezas de invaluable valor para asistir a los médicos en el encuentro clínico con pacientes, a ambos para tomar decisiones apropiadas relacionadas con el cuidado de la salud y proveen una estructura alternativa para la integración de la evidencia y aplicación de valores para alcanzar las recomendaciones de tratamiento. Pero en lugar de ser instrumentos precisos basados en evidencia, muchas veces están plagadas de recomendaciones de expertos, las que no solo les restan confiabilidad, sino que su aplicabilidad en otros escenarios, diferentes sistemas de salud, hospitales no universitarios, atención primaria, pacientes de diferentes latitudes, resulta ser muy complicada.
